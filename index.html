How it works: for aA + bB → …, comparing n(A) ÷ a with n(B) ÷ b tells you which reactant runs out first — this tool states that immediately rather than re-deriving it (that's what Stoichiomathics is for). Once it's known, every other species in the equation scales from the same "extent of reaction" — n(limiting) ÷ coef(limiting) — to give the amount of each product formed and how much of the excess reactant is left over.
Relative atomic masses follow common data-booklet values (H = 1.0, O = 16.0, Cl = 35.5, Cu = 63.5 …). State symbols and spectator H⁺/OH⁻ are omitted. Molar gas volume: 24.0 dm³ mol⁻¹ at RTP, 22.4 dm³ mol⁻¹ at STP. Always re-check against your own mark scheme.
